COS Training School resources available online

Please be advised that you can access the workshop recordings of the Training School sessions held in September 2021 at comfa.eu. The lecture series comprises the following topics essential to Core Outcome Set development:

  • Development and use of COS: principles and workflow (Karen Matvienko-Sikar)
  • Summary of outcome domains from literature (Jan Kottner)
  • Effective management of a COS development project (Nina Seylanova)
  • Qualitative research in COS development (Audrey Dunn Galvin)
  • Introduction to Systematic Reviews (Robert Boyle)
  • Bias and the RoB 2 Tool (Bartosz Helfer)
  • GRADE Guidelines (Derek Chu)

The course materials will serve as a basis for the novel COS Development Toolkit helping researchers plan and create a COS project step-by-step. Please reach out to Dr Nina Seylanova if the project has sparked your interest.

Reminder: Clinical and Experimental Allergy (CEA) special collection – invitation to publish

This is a gentle reminder of the opportunity to publish state-of-the-art research focussed on outcome research and patient-centered care in allergy and clinical immunology for a special collection in the Clinical & Experimental Allergy journal. We welcome the submission of State-of-the-Art Reviews, Systematic reviews, Hypothesis, Perspectives, Clinical Trials, and Original Research articles that will narrow the existing knowledge gaps in the field. Systematic reviews are particularly encouraged. Below are some suggested sub-topics to review in potential manuscripts:

  1. Cost-effectiveness analysis and outcome data demonstrating feasibility of clinical facilities, services and/or interventions
  2. Integrated care pathways and assessment of services delivery effectiveness and patient satisfaction
  3. Patient access to healthcare and/or disparity in healthcare delivery
  4. Patient-Centered Care and patient-reported outcome measures research
  5. Demonstrating evidence of need for a COS and/or identifying potential list of outcomes
  6. Development and/or validation of tools for use in research and clinical practice

Please respond to this email if you would like to discuss an idea, or initiate a project on one of the above mentioned topics.
